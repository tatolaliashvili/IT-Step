World of Warcraft

Set in Year 25 of the King's Calendar, Vanilla WoW thrust players into a fractured world teeming with localized threats that hinted at greater cosmic horrors. King Varian Wrynn vanished from Stormwind, leaving Highlord Bolvar Fordragon as regent—unbeknownst to all, manipulated by Onyxia, the black dragon scheming in human guise as Lady Katrana Prestor. Heroes began humbly: Alliance adventurers purged the Defias Brotherhood from Westfall's Deadmines, rebels born of stonemason betrayal. Horde warriors cleansed Orgrimmar's Ragefire Chasm of shadowy cults. Dungeons like the Wailing Caverns (corrupted druids), Shadowfang Keep (werewolves), and Blackfathom Deeps (naga and Old Gods' whispers) revealed festering corruptions. Escalation came with Blackrock Mountain. Blackrock orcs, slaves to the fire elemental lord Ragnaros—summoned centuries ago by Dark Iron dwarves—raided the surface world. Players delved Molten Core to slay Ragnaros, then ascended to Blackwing Lair, confronting Nefarian, Deathwing's son experimenting with chromatic dragons. Onyxia fell in her lair beneath Stormwind, exposing Varian's kidnapping and restoring the king. In the south, Silithus swarmed with silithid insects, puppets of the qiraji empire and the Old God C'Thun, imprisoned since the War of the Shifting Sands. A world event, the Gates of Ahn'Qiraj, saw Alliance and Horde unite in the Might of Kalimdor, amassing resources to breach the Scarab Wall. Raids on Ruins of Ahn'Qiraj and Temple of Ahn'Qiraj ended with C'Thun's death, but his whispers lingered. Finally, Naxxramas, the Scourge's floating necropolis, hovered over cities, led by lich Kel'Thuzad. Players stormed it, purging undead remnants of the Lich King—Arthas Menethil, now fused with the orc shaman Ner'zhul atop Northrend's Frozen Throne. Victory was pyrrhic; tensions boiled in battlegrounds like Warsong Gulch, and Lord Kazzak reopened the Dark Portal in Hellfire Peninsula, heralding doom. Azeroth stabilized, but the portal's glow promised invasion.

The Burning Crusade

One year later (Year 26), demons poured from the Dark Portal. Heroes charged through to Outland—once Draenor, shattered by Ner'zhul's portals during the Second War. Ruled by Illidan Stormrage, the "Betrayer" (exiled night elf demon hunter), Outland was a demonic playground. Alliance gained draenei allies—exiled eredar led by Prophet Velen, who fled Sargeras's corruption of their world Argus millennia ago, crash-landing on Draenor before the orcish Horde's genocide. Horde welcomed blood elves, high elf survivors of Arthas's Sunwell defilement, addicted to magic and following Prince Kael'thas Sunstrider to Illidan's side. In Hellfire Peninsula and beyond, players battled fel orcs from Magtheridon's Hellfire Citadel, broken draenei, and Legion felguards. Shattrath's naaru (holy energy beings) offered sanctuary via Aldor or Scryer factions. Key raids: Karazhan (Medivh's haunted tower, time anomalies); Gruul's Lair and Magtheridon's Lair (ogres and pit lord); Serpentshrine Cavern (Lady Vashj's naga); Tempest Keep (Kael'thas's stolen naaru ship); Black Temple (Akama's broken draenei betray Illidan); Zul'Aman (troll loa); and Sunwell Plateau, where Kael'thas summoned Kil'jaeden himself. Heroes slew Illidan, purified the Sunwell with Velen's aid, and reclaimed Outland—reconnecting Horde orcs with noble Mag'har under Garrosh Hellscream. Yet, as Outland fell silent, a chill wind blew from the north...

Wrath of the Lich King

Year 27: The Lich King unleashed his Scourge on Azeroth—zombies overrunning cities, vrykul awakening from slumber. Heroes sailed to frozen Northrend, establishing bases in Borean Tundra and Howling Fjord. New playable death knights—former Scourge champions freed at Acherus: The Ebon Hold—joined both factions. Argent Crusade (Tirion Fordring's holy order) and Knights of the Ebon Blade (Darroth's death knights) forged uneasy alliances. Threats abounded: mad blue Dragon Aspect Malygos assaulted magic users (raids: Nexus, Eye of Eternity); ice trolls in Zul'Drak; proto-dragons and tuskarr struggles. Raids escalated: Reliquary of Souls, Magisters' Terrace (Sunwell cleanup); Ulduar (titan city, Old God Yogg-Saron's madness, Algalon's planetary reset averted); Naxxramas redux; Icecrown Citadel, climax atop the Frozen Throne. Arthas, crowned by Frostmourne, fell to the Ashbringer-wielding Tirion and players. Highlord Bolvar Fordragon, charred and willing, donned the Helm of Domination, becoming the new Lich King to contain the Scourge. Azeroth endured, but the Lich King's echo—and Old Gods' whispers—promised more wars. This trilogy forged WoW's golden age, blending personal heroism with world-shaking stakes.
